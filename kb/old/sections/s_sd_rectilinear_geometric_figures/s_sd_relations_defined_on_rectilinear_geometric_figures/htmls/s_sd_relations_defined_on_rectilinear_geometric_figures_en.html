Relationships defined on the set of rectilinear shapes:

  • Tangent is a straight line which have one common point which circle and lying in it’s plane.

  • Secant line - in relation to two definite straight lines called straight line which cross these lines.

  • Parallel straight lines are straight lines in a plane which do not meet.

  • Perpendicular straight lines are straight lines which meet at a right angle.

  • The same direction, or codirectional called two rays (or two half-straight lines), if it’s possible to combine them by parallel transfer.

  • The boundary point (of the shape) is a geometric point that in any neighborhood of this point there is a geometric point belonging to the figure, and the point does not belong to this figure.
  • Additional rays is called different rays of one straight line which have common initial point.
  • Contrary directed rays is called two rays (or two half-straight lines), if each of them have the same direction with the ray (or two half-straight lines), an additional ray to another.

  • Midpoint of segment is a point which divides the segment into two equal segments.

  • Length - numerical characteristic spread of the object.
