Definition. Segment is part of a straight line, which are lying between two definite points of this straight line, including these points.

If segment is a part of a straight line (or a part of a ray), then the segment includes into the straight line (or a part of the ray).

Two end points, which are limit segment, are called boundary point. Interior points of segment are locketed between is's boundary points.

Designation. Segment is denoted by symbols of it's boundary points, that is the segment, which have boundary points A and B, is denoted by symbol AB.

Each segment has a length expressed by a positive number. The length of the segment is equal to the sum of the lengths of the parts into which it divided by any of it's interior point. The length of the segment AB is denoted as |AB|. If point A and B coincids, then the segment is equal to zero.

To measure the segment means to compare it with other segment which length taken as a unit of length.

Equal (congruent) segments are referred to such segments which can be overlaid on top of each other so that their ends coincide. Equal (congruent) segments have the same length.

Longer segment of two segments is called such segment which length is greater.

The ratio of the lengths of any two segments does not depend on the choice of units.

Proportional to the segments are called segments, which lengths are proportional.

Middle of segment - is a point which divides the segment into two equal segments.
