Definition. Stright line - is perfectly flat and unbounded line, infinitely extended in two directions.

Designation. Stright line is often denoted lowercase letters: a, b, …, or two uppercase letters AB.

Axioms of belonging points to a stright line:

  • Through any two points in space, it’s possible to draw the only one stright line.

  • At least two points are on every stright line.

  • There are three points at least, which don’t lie on a stright line.

The axioms of the reciprocal position of points on the stright line:

  • If one of three points lies between two other, then all three points lie on one stright line.

  • For two any points exist the third point, which lies between a pair of points.

  • From three points of a stright line the only one points lies between a pair of points.

The axiom of the attachment of the stright line to plane:

If two points of the stright line are in the plane, then all points of the stright line are in this plane.
